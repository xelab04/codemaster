Frequently Asked Questions

> How do you register?
> No need to register in advance! When the qualifiers open on Friday, you will be prompted for your name, school and email address, meaning the registration is done automatically as you compete in the qualifiers.
> Is participation on a solo basis?
Yes. Participation is not done on a group level in this edition of CodeMaster.
> What are the online qualifiers like?
It will be a set of close-ended questions set in Google Sheets. The questionnaire will be randomised to avoid cheating. The form will begin accepting responses on Friday 26th of May at 10:00 and will last until Saturday 27th, at 22:00.
> Will we get a certificate of participation?
Absolutely! Finalists will get a printed certificate while those who didn't make it past the qualifiers will receive theirs in digital format.
> What do we need to code?
The finals will comprise of down-to-earth questions set in a realistic scenario. We won't be asking you to implement a certain algorithm, but rather come up and code your own approach to the problem. There is no single right answer!
> Do I need a laptop to participate?
Yes. The finals will require actual coding. Finalists will have to bring their [charged] laptops, or tablets. IDE's and development environments are the students' responsibilities.
> What if my laptop runs out of charge?
You are requested to have your laptop already charged. Charging points will be few and far between. Running out of charge is the students' responsibility.
> Can I access Stack Overflow or Chat GPT?
While we understand that professionals use these powerful tools to aid in coding, we will not allow access to the internet during the finals. This is to ensure fairness and that there is no remote teamwork through the internet.
